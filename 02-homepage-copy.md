## FAQ

Gold tag: Common Questions
H2: Everything You Need to Know

Q: Do I need to de-register from my NHS GP?
No. We work entirely alongside the NHS. You keep your NHS GP — we're an additional option for when you need to be seen sooner, or need more time.

Q: When can I get an appointment?
Every Friday we run a full clinic from 9am to 8pm. All other days are available by appointment only. We're working towards a full seven-day service in the coming months.

Q: Are prescriptions really included?
The prescription writing is included — if the doctor decides you need medication, he writes the prescription as part of your appointment at no extra charge. You then take it to any UK pharmacy and pay only the dispensing cost there. Medication charges are set by the pharmacy, not by us.

Q: Are referrals included too?
Yes. If you need referring to a specialist, the doctor will write the letter as part of your appointment — no separate charge, no extra visit needed.

Q: Can children be seen?
Yes. Children's appointments are available from £49 for a 15-minute consultation. We see patients of all ages.

Q: Do you offer blood tests and scans?
Yes. Blood tests and scans are available and will be quoted separately depending on what is needed. The doctor will discuss this with you during your appointment.

Q: What if I need to see a specialist?
The doctor can refer you to private specialists and arrange scans to help fast-track your care where needed. The referral letter is included in your consultation fee.

Q: Do you offer ongoing care for chronic conditions?
Absolutely. Many patients come to us for ongoing management of conditions like hypertension, diabetes, and thyroid disorders — and value seeing the same doctor at every visit.
