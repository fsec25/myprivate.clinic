# Content Pillars for Dr FS — LinkedIn Strategy

---

## Pillar 1: Access, Private Care, and Lessons from Building a Clinic
**Angle:** Build in Public
**Purpose:** Make his current journey visible and commercially relevant.

Topics:
- Why access matters
- What patients are really paying for in private primary care
- What it takes to build a clinic from scratch
- Lessons from the CQC journey
- Challenges in modern UK primary care

> This is likely his strongest pillar right now.

---

## Pillar 2: Frontline GP Realities
**Purpose:** Establish clinical credibility and human warmth.

Topics:
- What general practice really looks like
- The range of problems GPs solve
- Uncertainty in primary care
- What good consultations require
- What patients often misunderstand

> This gives him medical authority.

---

## Pillar 3: Health Tech, AI, and Innovation Through a Clinic Builder Lens
**Purpose:** Attract startups, operators, and innovation people.

Topics:
- Where AI could genuinely help doctors
- What health-tech founders misunderstand about care delivery
- What clinicians can contribute beyond medicine
- Thoughts on workflow, adoption, change management
- Experimenting with tools, agents, automation

> Important: this pillar must stay grounded and specific, not generic futurism.

---

## Pillar 4: Community, Prevention, and Health Literacy
**Purpose:** Show values, public trust, and long-term vision.

Topics:
- Community education
- Preventive health
- Culturally aware care
- What people should know before they become unwell
- Better health habits in Gujarati / South Asian communities

> This makes him more than "doctor + business." It gives moral depth.

---

## Optional Personality Layer (Recurring Tone, Not a Full Pillar)
**Life, curiosity, and personality**

Examples:
- Wanting patients to laugh once
- Trying horse riding for the fun of it
- Building because he enjoys the thrill
- Valuing time with his children

> Not fluffy. Human. This will make him memorable.

---

## Positioning

**How he should be described:**
- Multidisciplinary thinker
- Clinician with an entrepreneurial lens
- Broad operator across care, business, and innovation
- Someone comfortable across patient care, systems, and building

**Avoid:** "AI expert"
**Better:** exploring AI in healthcare / early adopter / clinician interested in practical applications / learning by building and testing

---

## Positioning Statement Options

**Option 1 — strongest all-rounder**
> GP | Building at the intersection of patient care, private healthcare, and health innovation

**Option 2 — warmer and more human**
> General Practitioner with an entrepreneurial lens on healthcare, access, and better patient experiences

**Option 3 — startup-friendly**
> Clinician, builder, and early adopter exploring how better systems and better tools can improve care

**Option 4 — slightly more premium/polished**
> GP focused on access, innovation, and building better models of care

**Recommended:** Option 1 or 4.

## Tone Guidance

He should sound:
- Intelligent but not self-important
- Curious but not gimmicky
- Entrepreneurial but not "hustle"
- Human, warm, and lively
- Optimistic and forward-looking
- Fun — not silly, but alive, not stiff

---

## Strategic Intent

LinkedIn's role for Dr FS:
- Establish authority
- Make him look credible and current
- Document his thinking
- Create serendipity for future opportunities
- Support the broader brand around the clinic

Build with the expectation of:
- Compounding trust
- Consistency
- Brand recall
- Cross-channel coherence

Not chasing vanity metrics.
