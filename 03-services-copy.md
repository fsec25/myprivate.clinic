# 7. WEIGHT LOSS MANAGEMENT

---

Gold tag: Weight Loss · My Private Clinic
H1: Lose Weight With Medical Support Behind You

Subheadline:
Clinically supervised weight loss with Dr Faizal Secretary. Medical assessment, prescription weight loss medication where appropriate, and ongoing support — in Preston, without the wait.

---

The Problem With Doing It Alone

Losing weight is not simply a matter of willpower. For many people, biology works against them. Hormones that regulate hunger, metabolism that resists change, a body that holds on to weight even when you're doing everything right.

If you've tried diets, exercise programmes and supplements and found the results disappointing — that's not a personal failing. It's physiology. And it's exactly why medical supervision exists.

---

What We Offer

A proper clinical weight loss programme, led by Dr Faizal Secretary. Not a slimming club. Not an online questionnaire followed by a courier delivery. A qualified GP who assesses you properly, prescribes safely, and monitors your progress in person.

---

The Medications

Where clinically appropriate, the doctor can prescribe the latest GLP-1 weight loss medications.

Semaglutide (Wegovy)
A once-weekly injection that mimics a hormone your body naturally produces after eating. It slows digestion, reduces appetite and helps you feel full sooner and for longer. Clinical trials show average weight loss of 15% of body weight over 68 weeks.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro)
A dual-action once-weekly injection targeting two hormones involved in appetite regulation. Clinical trials show average weight loss of 20–22% of body weight — the most effective prescription weight loss medication currently available.

Both medications are self-administered once weekly via a small pre-filled pen. The doctor will show you exactly how to use it.

Medication is dispensed from a pharmacy at pharmacy cost — not marked up by us.

---

Who This Is For

You may be a good candidate if:
- Your BMI is 30 or above, or
- Your BMI is 27 or above with a weight-related health condition such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure or high cholesterol
- You have tried to lose weight through diet and exercise without sufficient success
- You want medical supervision rather than an online service with no clinical oversight

---

What's Included

Initial Consultation
A thorough 30-minute appointment covering your weight history, medical history, current medications, lifestyle and goals.

Personalised Plan
Medication is one part of the programme. The doctor will also discuss dietary changes, activity and lifestyle.

Follow-Up Appointments
Regular check-ins to review progress, adjust dosage, manage any side effects and keep you on track.

---

What It Is Not

Medical weight loss is not a quick fix. The medications work best alongside changes to diet and lifestyle. The doctor will be honest with you about what to expect.

---

Pricing:
Initial consultation (30 minutes): £120
Follow-up appointment: £120
Weight loss medication: dispensed from pharmacy at pharmacy cost
Blood tests (if required): quoted separately

---

How It Works

Step 1 — Book Your Consultation
A 30-minute appointment. Come prepared to talk about your health history, what you've tried before, and what you want to achieve.

Step 2 — Assessment
The doctor assesses your suitability, explains your options clearly. You leave with everything you need to get started.

Step 3 — Ongoing Support
Follow-up appointments to monitor progress, adjust dosage and ensure the medication is working safely.

---

FAQ:

Q: Is prescription weight loss medication safe?
GLP-1 medications are licensed in the UK and have been through rigorous clinical trials. Like all medications they have potential side effects — the most common being nausea, particularly in the early weeks. The doctor will discuss these fully.

Q: How much weight can I expect to lose?
Clinical trials show average weight loss of 15% with semaglutide and up to 20–22% with tirzepatide, when combined with lifestyle changes. Individual results vary.

Q: Do I have to inject myself?
Yes. Both medications are given as a once-weekly self-injection using a small pre-filled pen. The doctor will demonstrate exactly how to use it.

Q: How much does the medication cost?
Medication costs are set by the pharmacy. The doctor will give you a clear indication at your consultation.

Q: What happens when I stop taking the medication?
Weight can return when medication is stopped, particularly without sustained lifestyle changes. The doctor will discuss a long-term plan at your follow-up appointments.

Q: Can I get this medication online without seeing a GP?
Yes, but we'd encourage caution. Online services often rely on questionnaires rather than clinical assessments. Medical weight loss is a clinical intervention — it deserves clinical supervision.

Q: Is everyone suitable?
No. GLP-1 medications are not suitable for people with certain medical conditions. The doctor will review your full medical history before prescribing.

Allergy vs Intolerance — What's the Difference?

A true allergy involves your immune system producing IgE antibodies in response to a substance. Reactions can range from mild (sneezing, rash) to severe (anaphylaxis). IgE blood testing identifies true allergic responses accurately.

A food intolerance does not involve the immune system in the same way. It produces slower symptoms — bloating, fatigue, skin issues. Many widely marketed food intolerance tests (IgG testing) are not clinically validated. The doctor will be straight with you about what the evidence says.

FAQ:

Q: What is specific IgE blood testing?
It measures the level of IgE antibodies your immune system produces in response to particular allergens. A positive result indicates a true allergic sensitisation. It is the most clinically validated method of allergy testing.

Q: Is this the same as a skin prick test?
No. Skin prick testing is carried out by allergy specialists. IgE blood testing via blood sample is equally reliable. If skin prick testing is more appropriate, the doctor will refer you to a specialist.

Q: Can you test for food intolerances?
Food intolerances are not reliably diagnosed by blood testing. The doctor will discuss your symptoms honestly and advise on the most appropriate approach, including elimination diets where relevant.

Q: How many allergens can be tested at once?
Multiple allergens can be tested from a single blood sample. The doctor will discuss which panel is most appropriate.

Q: What happens if I test positive for an allergy?
The doctor will explain your results clearly, advise on allergen avoidance, and prescribe medication or arrange specialist referral where needed.

Q: Do I need to stop antihistamines before the test?
No. Unlike skin prick testing, IgE blood testing is not affected by antihistamines.
